Your Metabolism Type

The Cortisol Burnout

Your metabolism isn't slow — it's been hijacked by stress hormones. The harder you push, the more your body holds on. This isn't a willpower problem. It's a cortisol problem.

What's actually happening
You're managing a career, kids' schedules, the mental load of running a household — and then layering on restrictive diets and hard workouts on top of that. Here's the problem: your body is already running on elevated cortisol. When you add more restriction and more cardio, you compound the stress on a system that's already maxed out. Cortisol tells your body it's in danger — so it holds onto fat, especially around your belly, for emergency fuel. The afternoon crash, the sugar cravings, the wired-but-tired feeling at night? All cortisol. The good news: this responds powerfully and quickly to the right approach.

3pm energy crash
Wired at night, tired all day
Sugar & carb cravings
Belly fat that won't move
Cardio not helping
Mood swings & bloating
Why what you've tried hasn't worked More cardio raises cortisol further — it's actively working against you. Low-carb diets spike cortisol too. You've been following advice designed for a 25-year-old with normal hormone levels. The Metabolic Priming Method™ is built specifically for THIS.
Your move this week
One action step
Replace one cardio session with a 30-minute strength training workout and eat 25–30g of protein within an hour of waking. This alone starts shifting your cortisol pattern and signals your metabolism it's safe to let go of fat.

Your Metabolism Type

The Metabolic Adaptation Type

Your metabolism hasn't broken down — it's adapted to protect you. Years of under-eating and overtraining have taught your body to survive on less. The fix isn't more restriction. It's a full reset.

What's actually happening
After years of dieting, your metabolism has learned to function on very little food. It's not broken — it's adapted to protect you from what it perceives as a famine. When you eat less, your body slows everything down: metabolism, thyroid function, energy output. Add hormone shifts from perimenopause and you get brain fog, feeling cold, low motivation, and weight gain even when you're barely eating. This is metabolic adaptation — and it's incredibly common in high-achieving women who've spent years being "good." The answer is counterintuitive: you likely need to eat MORE, not less.
Signs you're this type
Low energy all day
Gaining weight eating less
Brain fog & low motivation
Feel cold often
Hair or skin changes
Tried "everything" — nothing works
Why what you've tried hasn't worked Every restrictive diet made this worse. Your doctor ran basic labs, said "you're fine," and told you to eat less and move more. But basic labs don't show metabolic adaptation or cortisol dysregulation. You're not crazy — your symptoms are real. And there's a real fix.
Your move this week
One action step
Start eating MORE — specifically protein. Aim for at least 100g of protein per day and add two strength sessions this week. Under-eating is likely slowing your metabolism further. Your body needs a clear signal that food is safe and that building muscle is the goal — not surviving a diet.
